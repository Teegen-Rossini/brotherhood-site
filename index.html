how it works

a daily ten-minute window.

No schedules to manage and nothing to keep up with. When the window opens, everyone's phone says so at the same moment.

1

the crew forms

Start a crew, share one invite code with your people. Eight, tops. No search, no suggestions, no strangers.

2

the window opens

Once a day, at a time you won't predict, Breaktime begins — ten minutes for messages, voice notes, photos, and one-tap crew calls.

3

then it closes

When it's done, it's done. No feed to scroll back into, no red dots begging you back. Tomorrow there'll be another window.

who a crew is

any crew you refuse to lose.

It's called Brotherhood, but a crew is simply the small group of friends you won't let drift — two to eight of them, whoever they are.

the old crew

School mates, uni friends, the team from the best season of your life. The group chat that went quiet — give it a heartbeat.

you two

A crew can be two. You and your partner, across a long distance or a long week — ten minutes that don't get eaten by logistics.

the girls

The friends who've seen every era of you and still show up. Same window, same ten minutes, all of you at once.

the family

Siblings, cousins, mum and dad — scattered across cities and time zones. One shared window a day beats a silent family chat.

why ten minutes is enough

the eight-minute rule.

Researchers found that a call as short as eight minutes is enough to make someone feel measurably less alone — and that what keeps friendships alive isn't grand gestures, it's small, regular contact. Some friends keep it as a pact: "got eight minutes?" means you pick up.

Brotherhood builds that into the day. The window gives your crew ten — eight for the rule, two for the nonsense.

when it can't wait

emergency meetings.

Some news can't sit until tomorrow's window. Got engaged, got fired, got scared, got the results — any member can call an emergency meeting: an extra ten-minute window that starts the moment you sound it, and every phone in the crew goes off at once.

Everyone gets one a week, so it never turns into noise. When the alarm goes, it means someone needs the crew — and the crew shows up.

contact

talk to a human.

Questions, trouble, or something to report — write to brotherhood.support.app@gmail.com and a person will answer. If the link doesn't open your mail app, just copy the address.
